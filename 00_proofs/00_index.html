Methods of Proving

In Geometry, the most important process you should understand is starting with a given statement and proving its conclusion. To illustrate many of these examples, we will be using basic discrete mathematics and basic number theory in order to provide a foundation and intuition for the geometry to follow. The examples in this section will illustrate uses of a direct proof, which start with a given hypothesis and arrive at its conclusion. Additional tools introduced at your disposal for directly proving a proposition include Proof by Cases (also called Proof by Exhaustion) and Proof by Induction. Another way to prove is by an indirect proof, which starts from some form of the negation of the conclusion. One can start from a Proof by Contradiction or by Proof by Contrapositive. However, some of the examples involved in these sections assume you fully understand how to perform a Direct Proof. It is recommended to fully understand the Direct Proof and the tools and techniques associated with it. Then to move on to Indirect Proofs, which can be a understandably confusing.
